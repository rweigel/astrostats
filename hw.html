4.3 The Poisson Distribution

510

The Poisson distribution can be derived as a limit of the Binomial distribution. This is also discussed in Devore 3.6.

512

If

514

  1. in a sufficiently short amount of time, Δt, only 0 or 1 event can occur (two or more simultaneous events are impossible);

    516

  2. the probability of exactly 1 event occurring in Δt is equal to λΔt, where λ is a constant; and

    517

  3. any non-overlapping intervals of length Δt are independent Bernoulli trials,

    518
516-518

the probability of k events occurring in the time interval t=NΔt is

520

P(k)=k!(λt)ke−λt​

522

for sufficiently large N. Another way of writing this equation is, if p is the probability of event in time Δt, and, by definition, λ≡p/Δt, then

524

P(k)=k!(pΔtt​)ke−pΔtt​​

526

Next, using the definition t≡NΔt,

528

P(k)=k!(pN)ke−pN​
